Madelyn Andersen

I am a PhD Student in Bayesian Statistics at MIT, passionate about advancing innovative statistical methods and translating complex algorithms into accessible tools. I thrive on transforming challenging problems into creative solutions—whether in the lab, on the ballroom dance floor, or while training dogs in high-energy sports.

My journey in research and technology has instilled in me a deep appreciation for precision, creativity, and collaboration. I continually seek opportunities to push boundaries and develop solutions that have real-world impact.

Research

My research currently focuses on improving variational inference methods. I integrate techniques from machine learning, probabilistic programming, and statistical theory to bridge the gap between abstract theory and practical application.
